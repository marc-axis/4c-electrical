Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our electrical services in the Garden Route.

4C Electrical provides electrical services across the full Garden Route — including George, Mossel Bay, Knysna, Wilderness, Sedgefield, Hartenbos, Great Brak River, and Albertinia in the Western Cape, South Africa. Contact us to confirm availability for your specific location.

A Certificate of Compliance (COC) is a legal document issued by a registered electrician confirming that an electrical installation meets SANS 10142 — the South African standard for the wiring of premises. A COC is required by law when selling a property, connecting a new electricity supply, or after major electrical work. 4C Electrical issues COCs for all qualifying installations across the Garden Route.

A Variable Speed Drive (VSD) — also called a variable frequency drive or inverter — is an electronic device that controls the speed of an electric motor by varying the voltage and frequency of the power supply. VSDs are used in industrial and commercial settings to reduce energy consumption, protect motors from mechanical stress, and enable precise process control. Common applications include pumps, fans, compressors, and conveyor systems. 4C Electrical installs and commissions VSDs across the Garden Route.

Yes. 4C Electrical installs both grid-tied and off-grid solar PV systems for residential and commercial properties across the Garden Route. Grid-tied systems feed excess power back to the grid, while off-grid systems provide full energy independence.
